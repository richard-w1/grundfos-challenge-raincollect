The RainUSE Nexus

The Challenge: Commercial and Industrial buildings are facing a perfect storm: rising water utility costs, stricter environmental regulations, and increasing threats from floods and water scarcity. Grundfos has the technology to solve this, but finding the right buildings at the right time is like finding a needle in a haystack.

Your challenge is to build a web-based prototype that acts as an automated prospecting engine by state in the continental US. 

First, your app must use computer vision and satellite data to identify physical targets: specifically calculating commercial roof catchment areas (flagging those >100,000 sq ft) and detecting the presence of large cooling towers, outputting a Confidence Score for these visual detections.

Next, the engine must cross-reference these physical attributes with multiple financial and environmental data streams. The winning solution will ingest all this data to calculate a final, holistic Viability Score for specific buildings, proving both the environmental impact and the business ROI for a Grundfos water reuse system.

Key Variables to Target: 

Physical (AI/CV): Roof catchment area (>100,000 sq ft), cooling tower presence, and detection Confidence Scores.

Financial & Regulatory: High utility costs, tax incentives, and local regulatory drivers (e.g., stormwater fees)

Corporate: Corporate sustainability (ESG/LEED) goals and climate risk profiles.

Suggested Open Datasets: 

Spatial/Visual: Google Earth Engine, Sentinel-2 / Landsat satellite imagery, Open Buildings Dataset.

Environmental/Utility: US EPA Water Data, local municipal open data portals (for utility rates/tax incentives)

Corporate: SEC EDGAR database (for scraping corporate ESG reports and 10-K risk disclosures)






PURPOSE

The rainwater and stormwater management industry is constrained by high CAPEX, space limitations, lack of standardization, and market fragmentation, limiting growth to an 8% CAGR. We aim to disrupt the market with our innovative, compact solution that offers lower CAPEX and OPEX, along with several other significant benefits.

Water is one of the most overlooked sustainability frontiers and now, one of the most financially rewarding. Whether you’re capturing rooftop rain or recycling your shower water, these systems cut utility costs, reduce strain on infrastructure, and can earn businesses real incentives.

Dallas/Ft. Worth, receives approximately 34 inches of rain per year. With only 10 inches of rainfall a 10,000-sq. ft. roof could yield over 60,000 gallons therefore, businesses in areas where rainfall is 30 inches could yield over 180,000 gallons / year. Just think many buildings are well over 100,000-sq. feet!

Austin, TX and Tucson, AZ are ideal if you want to tie rainwater harvesting directly to cooling risk, water scarcity, and resilience for data centers or industrial loads. The incentives may be smaller than in areas like Philadelphia, PA but the strategic value per gallon is high. 

In conclusion, rainwater harvesting is an untapped potential for commercial and industrial businesses to truly act as water stewards. At Grundfos we believe there is a “Possibility in Every Drop”!
